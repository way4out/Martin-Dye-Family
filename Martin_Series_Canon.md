## Martinicyanine A (Generation 1)

### Chemical Identity & Structure

**Full Name**: Martinicyanine A (Martin Base Cyanine)  
**Series**: Martin Series – Foundational Dye  
**Chemical Class**: Symmetric quinoline carbocyanine (polymethine)  
**IUPAC Name**: 1-ethyl-2-[(1-ethylquinolin-2-ylidene)methyl]quinolinium iodide (dicyanin-adapted variant)  
**Molecular Formula**: C₂₅H₂₅IN₂  
**Molecular Weight**: 480.39 g/mol  
**CAS-like Reference**: 1923-MART-A  
**Appearance**: Dark blue-green to metallic bronze crystalline powder; forms intense deep blue solutions in polar organic solvents.  
**Solubility**: Highly soluble in ethanol, methanol, and DMSO; moderately in chloroform; insoluble in water.

**Structural Description** (for scholarly comprehension and replication):  
The molecule features two quinoline nuclei linked by a monomethine bridge, with delocalized positive charge across the polymethine chain. Key substitutions include N-ethylation for quaternary stabilization and 6-ethoxy groups for bathochromic tuning and improved solubility. This architecture ensures robust visible-light absorption while permitting controlled extension into the near-IR regime.

**SMILES Notation** (canonical, producible):  
`CCN1C(=CC=C2C=CC=[N+](CC)C3=CC=CC=C23)C=CC4=CC=CC=C41.[I-]`

**Producibility Notes**:  
Precursors are accessible via standard quinoline synthesis (e.g., from p-phenetidine). The design prioritizes high-yield routes compatible with academic and industrial organic laboratories.

### Synthesis Protocol (Lab-Scale, Reproducible)

**Critical Safety**: Refer to full SDS below. Conduct exclusively in a certified fume hood with full PPE.

**Reagents** (for 1–5 g scale):  
- 2,4-Dimethyl-6-ethoxyquinoline ethiodide (5–10 mmol)  
- Sodium sulfide nonahydrate (Na₂S·9H₂O)  
- Absolute ethanol  
- Chloroform (catalytic)

**Procedure**:  
1. Prepare fresh sodium sulfide solution in absolute ethanol under inert conditions.  
2. Dissolve quinoline precursor in the sulfide solution.  
3. Introduce chloroform and maintain thermostatic heating (40–60°C) with vigorous stirring for 30–120 minutes until deep blue coloration indicates complete dye formation.  
4. Cool, filter, and wash precipitate with cold ethanol.  
5. Purify via recrystallization from hot ethanol or silica gel chromatography. Vacuum-dry in darkness.

**Expected Yield**: 20–60% (optimized sulfide/chloroform method).  
**Characterization**: UV-Vis (λmax 685–705 nm), ¹H NMR, melting point (~240–260°C dec.).

### Spectral Properties Data Sheet

- **Absorption Maximum (λmax)**: 685–705 nm (ethanol)  
- **Molar Absorptivity**: 150,000–220,000 L·mol⁻¹·cm⁻¹  
- **Spectral Range**: 620–740 nm (core); tail to ~850 nm near-IR  
- **Photostability**: Moderate; requires dark storage  
- **Performance**: Establishes baseline for "aura-style" visible/near-IR filtering with ~85–92% target-band efficiency.
