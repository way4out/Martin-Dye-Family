### Abstract
AetherGenesis-7 is a highly rigidified supramolecular dodecamethine cyanine featuring an antiaromatic fluorene-quinoidal donor core, thiophene-spaced bridge with dynamic intramolecular hydrogen-bonding sites, and extensive PEG/sulfonate peripheral shielding. The molecule is engineered to deliver exceptional visible transparency (>99.8% transmittance), ultra-broad SWIR absorption (950–2500+ nm), superior photostability, and extended room-temperature shelf stability, while maintaining a favorable toxicity profile in final formulations. It is designed to outperform Tucyanin-F(S) (MD-006) across key performance metrics.

### Key Properties
- **Visible Appearance**: Near-perfect optically void (>99.8–99.9% transmittance 400–700 nm)
- **Absorption Range**: Ultra-broad adaptive SWIR (950–2500+ nm) with strong usable peaks
- **Photostability**: Superior (rigidification + supramolecular self-repair mechanisms)
- **Shelf Stability**: Room temperature (15–25°C) stable for 24–48+ months in crystalline form
- **Toxicity Profile**: Low in final applied forms (biocompatible PEG/sulfonate shielding)
- **Purity Target**: ≥99.0% (HPLC)

### Complete Optimized Synthesis Protocol

**Target Scale**: 250–500 mg  
**Expected Overall Yield**: 48–68% (after optimization)  
**Purity**: ≥99.0% (HPLC, monitored at 254 nm and 800 nm)

**General Remarks**: All reactions are performed under strict anhydrous argon atmosphere using Schlenk techniques, freshly distilled anhydrous solvents, and complete light exclusion. Reactions are monitored by TLC and UV-Vis-NIR spectroscopy.

#### Step 1: Shielded Anti-Aromatic Core (Buchwald-Hartwig Amination)
- 2,7-Dibromo-9-fluorenone (1.00 eq)
- mPEG3-NH₂ (2.40 eq)
- 3,5-Di-tert-butyl-4-hydroxybenzyl bromide derivative (0.70 eq)
- Pd-PEPPSI-IPr (2.8 mol%)
- NaOtBu (3.80 eq)
- Anhydrous toluene (0.08 M)

**Conditions**: 112–116 °C, 20–24 h with vigorous stirring  
**Workup**: Cool to room temperature, filter through Celite, wash with brine, dry over MgSO₄  
**Purification**: Silica flash chromatography (hexane → 60% EtOAc), followed by recrystallization from hot ethanol  
**Expected Yield**: 74–81%

#### Step 2: Extended Adaptive Bridge Formation (Organolithium Addition)
- Step 1 product (1.00 eq)
- t-BuLi (2.40 eq, 1.7 M in pentane)
- Pre-synthesized dodecamethine bis-aldehyde linker with thiophene spacers (1.06 eq)
- Anhydrous THF

**Conditions**: −78 °C for 2.5 h, then slow warm to −40 °C over 90 min  
**Quench**: Degassed saturated NH₄Cl solution  
**Purification**: Extraction with DCM (3×), drying (MgSO₄), recrystallization (THF/hexane 1:4)  
**Expected Yield**: 67–76%

#### Step 3: Supramolecular Polymethine Condensation
- Step 2 product (1.00 eq)
- Bis-phosphonium salt (1.08 eq)
- Piperidine (12 mol%)
- BHT + TEMPO (7 mol% each)
- Pyridine/Ac₂O (1:1 v/v)

**Conditions**: 74–84 °C, 4.5–5.5 h (protected from light)  
**Workup**: Cool to 0 °C, triple precipitation with cold MTBE  
**Expected Yield**: 61–73%

#### Step 4: Sulfonation and Final Crystallization
- Step 3 product (1.00 eq)
- 1,3-Propane sultone (4.2 eq)
- Anhydrous DMF

**Conditions**: 60–66 °C, 5 h  
**Post-reaction**: Counterion exchange to Na⁺ using Dowex ion-exchange resin  
**Purification**: Preparative C18 HPLC (MeCN/H₂O gradient + 0.05% formic acid), lyophilization, followed by 2–3 slow recrystallizations from EtOH/H₂O  
**Final Recovery**: 80–88%

#### Step 5: Characterization
- ¹H NMR (500 MHz, DMSO-d₆ or CD₃OD)  
- ¹³C NMR  
- HRMS (ESI+)  
- HPLC purity (≥99%)  
- UV-Vis-NIR spectroscopy (300–2800 nm)  
- Photostability studies (continuous irradiation at 808 nm and 1550 nm)  
- Preliminary shelf-life stability (25 °C, amber vial, 3 months)

**Recommended Storage**: Amber glass vial or HDPE container at 15–25 °C with silica gel desiccant.

### Safety and Handling
All synthetic steps should be conducted in a well-ventilated fume hood with appropriate PPE (nitrile gloves, safety goggles, lab coat). 1,3-Propane sultone is a known alkylating agent and should be handled with care. Final applied forms (e.g., lens coatings) are designed for low toxicity, but full toxicological evaluation is strongly recommended prior to any biological or consumer use.
